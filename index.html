Our Story

2020 – Where It All Began

We met at Open Sky… and yes, she was my dance partner. Between awkward spins and stepping on each other’s feet, something magical happened. Turns out the real rhythm wasn’t in the music… it was in us.

2022 – The Plot Twist Year

We moved from Port Elizabeth to Pretoria, welcomed our son Carter, and got engaged — all in one whirlwind year! Chaos, love, and happiness guaranteed.

2024 – Home Sweet Forever

We bought our first family forever home together. The house where memories will be made… and maybe paint colour arguments too!

2026 – Officially Forever

After dance floors, moves, and sleepless nights, we finally say “I do.” Same team. Same love. Just upgraded to husband and wife.

Wedding Day Timeline

2:00 PM - Guests Arrive

Show up fashionably on time (or early if you're that aunt who needs the best seat). Grab a drink from the cash bar and mingle – but save the drama for after the vows.

2:30 PM - Ceremony Begins

The moment we've all been waiting for: Brenton tries not to faint, Nastassja floats down the aisle like a boss. Tears, rings, and "I do"s – keep tissues handy, folks.

3:30 PM - Cocktail Hour & Photos

Ceremony over! Time for bubbly (cash bar, remember?) and awkward family photos where everyone pretends to like each other. Smile through the chaos.

5:00 PM - Reception Starts & Dinner

Food time! Dig into the feast before the speeches turn everyone hangry. Pro tip: Pace yourself – dancing burns calories.

7:00 PM - Speeches & Toasts

Best man roasts Brenton, maid of honor embarrasses Nastassja. Laugh politely, even if the jokes are as old as our dance moves from 2020.

8:00 PM - First Dance

We hit the floor – hopefully without stepping on toes this time. Then it's your turn to boogie; no judgment if you dad-dance.

9:00 PM - Cake Cutting

We slice the cake without smashing it in each other's faces (maybe). Sweet treats for all – diabetic uncles, you've been warned.

10:00 PM - Party Time

DJ drops the beats, dance floor erupts. Last until 2 AM if you can; bonus points for surviving without blisters or regrets.

2:00 AM - Goodnight!

Party's over – go home, you're drunk (or just tired). Thanks for celebrating; drive safe or Uber it, no heroes.
